To meditate properly, sit comfortably in a quiet place, close your eyes, and focus your attention on your breath, gently bringing your mind back each time it wanders91013.

Key steps supported by expert guides:

  • Choose a calm space where you won’t be disturbed910.

  • Sit upright and comfortably—you can sit on a chair, cushion, or floor, as long as you feel stable and relaxed910.

  • Set a short time if you’re new, like 5–10 minutes, and gradually increase as you become more comfortable91013.

  • Focus on your breath. Direct your attention to the sensation of your breathing, either at your nose, chest, or belly91416.

  • Notice when your mind wanders. This is normal—when you catch yourself thinking about something else, gently return your focus to your breath without self-criticism9101314.

  • End with awareness. When your session ends, open your eyes slowly, and take a moment to notice how you feel physically and mentally913.

Additional tips for success:

  • Start with guided meditations if you’re unsure; audio or apps can help you build consistency14.

  • Try meditating at the same time each day to make it a habit1214.

  • Don’t worry about “emptying your mind”—the practice is simply bringing your attention back, over and over, with patience91413.

  • Experiment with different positions and times of day to find what suits you best1014.

There is no single “right” way to meditate; the most important principles are consistency, patience, and kindness toward yourself as you learn15149.
