Pneumonia is a lung infection that can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi.
Bacterial and viral pneumonia are typically contagious and can spread through the following ways:
  • Inhaling droplets from an infected person's cough or sneeze
  • Close contact with an infected person
  • Touching contaminated surfaces and then touching your eyes, nose, or mouth
Fungal pneumonia is generally not contagious, but it can be acquired through exposure to fungal spores in the environment.
The contagiousness of pneumonia depends on the type of infection and the severity of the illness.
Bacterial pneumonia is usually more contagious than viral pneumonia.
People with pneumonia are typically contagious for a few days to a few weeks.
It is important to stay home and avoid contact with others while you are sick to prevent spreading the infection.
If you think you may have pneumonia, it is important to see a doctor for diagnosis and treatment.
Early treatment can help improve your chances of recovery and prevent complications.
