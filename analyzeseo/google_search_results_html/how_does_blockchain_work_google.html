Blockchain is a decentralized, digital ledger that records transactions in a secure, transparent, and auditable way.
Show more
Show less
It works by creating blocks of data, linking them together in a chain, and distributing copies of the chain across a network of computers.
This distributed nature and cryptographic security make it difficult to tamper with or alter the recorded data.
Here's a more detailed breakdown:
Transactions and Blocks:
When a transaction occurs, it's recorded in a block along with other transactions.
Hashing and Linking:
Each block contains a unique hash, a cryptographic fingerprint, of its content and the hash of the previous block in the chain.
Network Distribution:
The new block, along with the updated chain, is then distributed to all nodes (computers) in the network.
Validation and Consensus:
Nodes validate the new block's data and, through a consensus mechanism, agree on its inclusion in the chain.
Immutable Record:
Once added, a block is virtually impossible to alter, creating a secure and auditable record of transactions.
Key Components of Blockchain Technology:
  • Distributed Ledger: The blockchain acts as a shared, distributed database of transactions.
  • Consensus Mechanism: The process by which nodes agree on the validity of new blocks and the order of the chain.
  • Smart Contracts: Programs stored on the blockchain that automatically execute when predetermined conditions are met.
Applications of Blockchain:
  • Cryptocurrency: Blockchain is the underlying technology for cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin.
  • Supply Chain Management: Tracking the movement of goods and ensuring transparency in supply chains.
  • Digital Identity: Creating secure and verifiable digital identities.
  • Healthcare: Storing and managing patient records securely.
  • Voting Systems: Creating transparent and verifiable voting systems.
