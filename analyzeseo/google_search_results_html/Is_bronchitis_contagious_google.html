Yes, bronchitis can be contagious.

Acute bronchitis, which is caused by a viral or bacterial infection, is contagious.
The virus or bacteria can spread through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks.
It can also be spread through contact with contaminated surfaces.
Chronic bronchitis, which is a long-term inflammation of the airways, is not usually contagious.
However, if it is caused by a bacterial infection, it can be contagious.
People with bronchitis should take precautions to prevent spreading the infection, such as:
  • Covering their mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing
  • Washing their hands frequently
  • Avoiding close contact with others
  • Staying home from work or school if they are sick

What are 5 symptoms of bronchitis?

The most common symptoms of bronchitis are:

Runny, stuffy nose.
Low-grade fever.
Chest congestion.
Wheezing or a whistling sound while breathing.
A cough that may produce yellow or green mucus (sputum)
Feeling run-down or tired.
