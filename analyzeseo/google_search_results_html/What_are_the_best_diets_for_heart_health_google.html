The best diet for heart health is one that is rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein.
Show more
Show less
This diet should also be low in saturated and trans fats, cholesterol, sodium, and added sugars.
Specific Dietary Recommendations:
  • Fruits and vegetables: Aim for at least 5 servings per day. Choose a variety of colors and types, including leafy greens, berries, and citrus fruits.
  • Whole grains: Include whole wheat bread, brown rice, quinoa, and oatmeal.
  • Lean protein: Choose fish, poultry, beans, lentils, and nuts.
  • Healthy fats: Include olive oil, avocado, and walnuts.
  • Low-fat dairy: Opt for low-fat or skim milk, yogurt, and cheese.
  • Limit: Saturated and trans fats (found in fatty meats, butter, and processed foods), cholesterol (from eggs and some meats), sodium (from processed foods and table salt), and added sugars (from sugary drinks, desserts, and snacks).
Examples of Heart-Healthy Diets:
  • Mediterranean diet: Emphasizes fruits, vegetables, fish, olive oil, and whole grains.
  • DASH (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) diet: Focuses on fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy, and whole grains while limiting sodium.
  • Plant-based diet: Excludes meat and emphasizes fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and nuts.
Additional Tips:
Cook at home more often to control ingredients, Read food labels carefully, Limit sugary drinks, Get regular exercise, and Manage stress effectively.
It's important to note that the best diet for heart health may vary depending on individual needs and preferences.
Consult with a healthcare professional for personalized guidance.
