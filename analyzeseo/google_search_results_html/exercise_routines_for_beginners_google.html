A beginner routine should include a mix of strength training and cardio, with a focus on exercises that target major muscle groups and improve cardiovascular fitness.
Strength Training for Beginners:
  • Full-Body Workouts: Target all major muscle groups 2-3 times a week, focusing on compound exercises like squats, deadlifts, push-ups, rows, and overhead presses.
  • Bodyweight Exercises: Start with bodyweight exercises like squats, lunges, push-ups, and planks to build strength and learn proper form.
  • Light Weights: Once comfortable with bodyweight exercises, gradually introduce light weights to increase the intensity.
  • Sets and Reps: Aim for 2-3 sets of 10-12 repetitions for each exercise.
  • Warm-up and Cool-down: Always include a warm-up before each workout and a cool-down afterward.
Cardio for Beginners:
  • Low-Impact Cardio: Start with low-impact activities like walking, jogging, or cycling for 30 minutes most days of the week.
  • Gradual Progression: Gradually increase the duration and intensity of your cardio workouts as you get fitter.
  • Listen to Your Body: Pay attention to how your body feels and take rest days when needed.
Example Beginner Workout Routine:
  • Day 1: Full-body strength training (squats, lunges, push-ups, rows, plank).
  • Day 2: Cardio (walking or jogging).
  • Day 3: Rest or active recovery (light stretching or a walk).
  • Day 4: Full-body strength training (focus on different exercises than Day 1).
  • Day 5: Cardio (cycling or another low-impact activity).
  • Day 6: Rest or active recovery.
  • Day 7: Rest.
Tips for Beginners:
  • Start Slow: Don't try to do too much too soon.
  • Prioritize Form: Proper form is crucial to prevent injuries.
  • Listen to Your Body: Rest and recovery are important for muscle growth and preventing injuries.
  • Find a Routine You Enjoy: Consistency is key to seeing results, so choose a routine that you find enjoyable and sustainable.
